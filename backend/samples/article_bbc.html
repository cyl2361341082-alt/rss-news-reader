Central banks slow down while markets keep climbing

Investors are entering a quieter but more complicated phase of the policy cycle.

Instead of preparing for rapid rate cuts, traders are now pricing a slower and more uneven path across major economies.

That shift has kept equity markets resilient, but it has also widened the gap between optimistic pricing and cautious central-bank messaging.

Markets are no longer moving on relief alone. They are moving on the belief that earnings can absorb higher rates for longer.

Portfolio managers say the result is a more selective rally, one that rewards durable cash flow and punishes balance sheets that still depend on cheap funding.
